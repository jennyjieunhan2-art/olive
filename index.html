What We Offer

Our Treatments

Comprehensive therapies rooted in Traditional Chinese Medicine, each tailored to your unique health goals.

01

Acupuncture

Using the twelve meridians to balance the body's Qi, acupuncture reduces inflammation, increases blood flow, and provides both immediate and long-term pain relief. A single-needle treatment often also includes cupping, gua-sha, or moxibustion as needed.

Learn More
02

Electro-Acupuncture

An FDA-approved device provides gentle, continuous electrical stimulation through acupuncture needles — improving healing of injured joints and muscles, increasing endorphin release, and promoting a sense of well-being and balanced nervous system function.

Learn More
03

Cupping Therapy

Small glass or plastic cups create a vacuum on your skin, stimulating circulation in the superficial muscle layers. Effective for arthritis, musculoskeletal pain, headaches, common cold, and cough. We also use "sliding cupping" over large areas like the back.

Learn More
04

Moxibustion

Therapeutic heat therapy using Mugwort (Artemisia Vulgaris). Heated herbs are applied directly or indirectly to specific acupuncture points, delivering deep therapeutic heat. Especially effective for cold and achy joints, scar tissue softening, immune boosting, and menstrual relief.

Learn More
05

Gua Sha

A traditional Chinese healing method in which a trained professional uses a smooth-edged tool to stroke the skin under gentle pressure. This raises small petechiae (rash-like dots) that indicate improved micro-circulation and begin a healing response in underlying tissues.

Learn More
06

Auricular Acupuncture

The ear is a complete microsystem of the human body. Needles, ear seeds, or magnets are applied to specific ear points to relieve pain, support addiction recovery (drug, alcohol, tobacco), PTSD and trauma, insomnia, stress, and a wide range of pain syndromes.

Learn More

Meet Your Practitioner

John Han, L.Ac.

Master of Oriental Medicine · South Baylo University

Growing up in South Korea, John was immersed in the rich cultural traditions of Oriental medicine, igniting a lifelong passion for this ancient healing art. He earned his Master's degree in Oriental Medicine from South Baylo University in Anaheim, California and holds a license from the California Acupuncture Board.

After completing his studies, John returned to Korea to deepen his clinical experience, joining a non-profit acupuncture association to volunteer services to underprivileged communities. Since 2008, he has been providing effective acupuncture treatments across Southern California — and now brings his expertise to Houston, Texas.

At Olive Acupuncture Clinic, John is dedicated to compassionate, patient-centered care — helping individuals achieve optimal health through a truly holistic approach.

Your First Visit

How It Works

We make your first visit easy, comfortable, and effective — from the moment you walk in.

01

Initial Paperwork

Start with a brief health history form. We ask about every aspect of your life to truly understand what might be going on — this is the holistic approach.

02

Consultation

We take time to discuss your main health concerns and goals. The practitioner will determine the best personalized treatment plan for your visit.

03

Treatment

Relax in a quiet, private room. We use a style of acupuncture requiring only access to feet, hands, and possibly ears. Just roll up your sleeves and socks.

04

Ongoing Care

For returning patients, we check in on your progress each visit and adjust the treatment. Modalities like cupping or gua-sha are included — no upselling.
