Our Mission

At Olive Acupuncture clinic in Houston, Texas, we follow a holistic approach to healthcare. We firmly believe in the body's inherent capacity to heal itself.

Our objective is to provide support and enhance this natural healing process through acupuncture and other holistic therapies. We are dedicated not only to alleviating the symptoms that brought you to us but also to addressing any underlying health issues that may be impacting your overall well-being.

Olive offers safe and effective therapies that maximize health and wellness benefits for patients while minimizing any associated risks.

Our Services

🎯

Pain Management

Acupuncture has been shown to be effective in pain management by relaxing muscle spasms, reducing local inflammation, improving blood flow and speeding healing. Our holistic approach may integrate acupuncture, cupping, massage, electrical stimulation, gua sha, dietary changes and herbal medicine.

🧘

Stress Relief & Fatigue

Acupuncture treatment allows the body and nervous system to unwind and recalibrate. With regular treatment, acupuncture can help restore balance and harmony to the body, reducing stress responses and allowing the body to settle into a healthier state.

🌿

Gut Health & Immune

We take a comprehensive approach to digestive and immune health, addressing the underlying causes of imbalance. Traditional Chinese Medicine considers the complex interplay between organs and meridians to restore health and vitality.

💆

Cupping Therapy

Cupping therapy is used to improve circulation, reduce pain and inflammation, and promote healing. It works alongside acupuncture as part of our comprehensive treatment approach.

✋

Gua Sha & Massage

These therapeutic techniques help improve blood flow, reduce muscle tension, and promote the body's natural healing processes as part of your personalized treatment plan.

🌱

Herbal Medicine

We recommend customized herbal formulations based on Traditional Chinese Medicine principles to support your treatment and enhance overall wellness.
